## Concepts

### Pivot Level as Resistance / Decision Node

- File: docs/concepts/pivot-level-as-resistance-decision-node.md
- Status: needs more evidence
- Source Scope: Initial extraction from a single Verified Investing / Gareth Soloway video across multiple examples and instruments
- Summary:
  Horizontal levels drawn from prior pivot highs or lows are treated as reference areas where price may act differently, including as targets, resistance, or decision points.
- Key Ambiguities:
  - Pivot-selection criteria are not defined.
  - Required number of pivots or touches to form a level is not defined.
  - Zone width and reaction definitions are not defined.
  - Role assignment between target, resistance, and decision point remains context-dependent.
- Next Validation Step:
  Collect 2–3 additional pivot-level examples across different instruments and record whether the level is presented as a cluster, single level, or zone.

### Trend Line Role Flip

- File: docs/concepts/trend-line-role-flip.md
- Status: needs more evidence
- Source Scope: Initial extraction from a single Verified Investing / Gareth Soloway video example on Russell 2000
- Summary:
  A diagonal trend line that previously interacted as support is later referenced as resistance after price breaks below it and retraces back to the line.
- Key Ambiguities:
  - Trend-line construction method is not defined.
  - Required touch count for line validity is not defined.
  - Break, retest, and rejection thresholds are not defined.
- Next Validation Step:
  Collect additional examples of broken trend-line retests and record whether the support-to-resistance role change is described consistently.
