# Concept: Pivot Level as Resistance / Decision Node

## 1. Description (Observation Only)

- Horizontal zones are drawn across prior pivot highs and prior pivot lows.
- Clusters of prior highs or lows are treated as a price band rather than a single exact price.
- Price approaches these horizontal levels after directional movement.
- The speaker identifies "low pivots... all through here" and marks a horizontal zone.
- Price moving into such a zone is described as reaching a "target."
- A similar level near `6800` on S&P 500 futures is described as "epic resistance."
- Prior pivot levels are also referenced on Google near `321` and on Russell 2000 near a double-top level around `76.40`.
- The charts show price reacting near prior pivot highs or lows.

## 2. Interpretation (Speaker Intent)

- The speaker treats prior pivot clusters as meaningful horizontal reference levels.
- The same type of level is used as a target, resistance, or decision point.
- The role assigned to the level appears to depend on context, including approach direction and prior movement.

## 3. Inputs

- Prior pivot highs.
- Prior pivot lows.
- Horizontal zones drawn across one or more prior pivots.
- Price clusters forming a band.
- Current price approaching the identified zone.
- Instrument and timeframe context shown on the chart.

## 4. Expected Behaviors

- Stall near the horizontal pivot zone.
- Rejection from the zone.
- Breakout through the zone.
- Breakdown through the zone.
- Continuation after interaction with the zone.
- Role shift between target, resistance, and decision point.

## 5. Invalidation Conditions

Not defined in source

## 6. Variations Observed

- Prior pivot cluster used as a target.
- Prior pivot cluster used as resistance.
- Prior pivot cluster used as a decision point.
- Prior pivot low referenced as a horizontal level.
- Prior pivot high or double-top area referenced as a horizontal level.

## 7. Testable Hypotheses

- When price approaches a horizontal zone defined by prior pivot clusters, the probability of a measurable reaction within a fixed tolerance increases relative to baseline approaches to non-marked price areas.
- When price approaches a prior pivot cluster from below, the probability of stalling or rejecting near the level increases relative to baseline upward approaches to non-pivot levels.
- When price reaches a prior pivot cluster identified as a target, the probability of a short-horizon directional change or pause increases relative to baseline after equivalent directional moves into non-pivot areas.

## 8. Measurement Considerations

- Timeframe must be recorded separately for intraday and daily charts.
- "Reaction" requires an explicit definition such as pause, reversal, rejection, breakout, or breakdown over a fixed lookahead window.
- Tolerance must be defined as zone width rather than assumed exact-price contact.
- Pivot-selection criteria are currently undefined and would need source-consistent specification before testing.
- Sample size should be segmented by instrument, timeframe, and approach direction.

## 9. Known Ambiguities / Gaps

- No explicit rule is given for how pivots are selected.
- No threshold is given for how many pivots or touches are required to form a level.
- No rule is given for zone width.
- No rule is given for when a level is treated as a target versus resistance versus decision point.
- No explicit distinction is given between reaction, reversal, and continuation.
- Mixed instruments and timeframes are shown without a source-stated normalization rule.
