# Concept: Trend Line Role Flip

## 1. Description (Observation Only)

- A diagonal trend line is drawn across multiple prior price interactions.
- Price had previously traded above the line.
- Price broke below the line.
- Price later retraced upward into the same line.
- The speaker states the Russell 2000 was likely to retrace to "this trend line."
- The speaker states: "This is going to act as resistance. Support. Support breaks down retraces."
- The speaker states that after price reaches the line, he expects rejection and movement back lower.
- The chart visually shows multiple prior interactions between price and the line before the retrace.

## 2. Interpretation (Speaker Intent)

- The speaker treats the diagonal line as support while price is above it.
- After price moves below the line, the same line is treated as resistance on retest.
- The retest is treated as a decision point where rejection is expected.

## 3. Inputs

- A diagonal trend line drawn from prior price interactions.
- Multiple prior price contacts with the line.
- Price position relative to the line before and after breakdown.
- A downward break below the line.
- A subsequent upward retrace into the same line.
- Instrument and timeframe context shown on the chart.

## 4. Expected Behaviors

- Retrace upward into the trend line after breakdown.
- Stall at the line.
- Rejection from the line.
- Movement lower after the retest.
- Role shift from support to resistance.

## 5. Invalidation Conditions

Not defined in source

## 6. Variations Observed

- Trend line used first as support and later as resistance.
- Retest of the broken line used as a decision point.

## 7. Testable Hypotheses

- When price breaks below a diagonal trend line with prior support interactions and later retraces to that line, the probability of a measurable rejection at the line increases relative to baseline retraces into non-marked diagonal areas.
- When price retests a previously supportive trend line from below after breakdown, the probability of short-horizon movement lower increases relative to baseline upward retraces that do not contact such a line.
- When price returns to a broken trend line from the opposite side, the probability of stalling near the line increases relative to baseline continuation through the same price path without a prior marked line.

## 8. Measurement Considerations

- Timeframe must be fixed for testing and should not mix intraday and higher-timeframe structures in the same sample.
- "Trend line" requires an explicit rule for how the line is drawn from observed price points.
- "Prior support interactions" requires an explicit count or qualification rule, which is not defined in the source.
- "Rejection" requires a fixed operational definition, such as reversal distance, failure to close through the line, or adverse movement over a fixed lookahead window.
- Tolerance is required because contact with a diagonal line may need a price-distance or percentage band rather than exact touch.
- Sample selection must distinguish first retest from repeated retests if both occur in the data.

## 9. Known Ambiguities / Gaps

- No explicit rule is given for how the trend line is constructed.
- No explicit rule is given for how many touches validate the line.
- No threshold is given for what qualifies as a break below the line.
- No threshold is given for what qualifies as a retest.
- No rule is given for when rejection is considered complete.
- No rule is given for when continuation through the line invalidates the role-flip interpretation.
