# Extraction Standard

## Core Distinctions

### Observation

- Records what is explicitly stated or visibly shown in the source.
- Must be traceable to a specific source segment, statement, or chart state.

### Interpretation

- Organizes or paraphrases source material without adding a new claim.
- May clarify wording, but does not establish facts beyond the source.

### Inference

- Adds implication, causality, intent, or meaning not explicitly provided by the source.
- Must not be recorded as observation.

## Minimum Extraction Requirements

Each extraction must include:

- source identification;
- timestamp or segment reference;
- exact language and/or clearly described chart behavior;
- chart context when relevant;
- enough detail to let a reviewer locate the same material.

Required chart context includes, when available and relevant:

- instrument or market referenced;
- timeframe;
- chart type or display context;
- visible labels or markers needed to interpret the observation.

If chart context materially affects meaning and is not recorded, the extraction is incomplete.

## Valid Observation Standard

A valid observation:

- is anchored to a specific source;
- uses exact language or a direct descriptive account of what is shown;
- excludes unstated explanation, prediction, recommendation, and persuasion;
- remains separable from interpretation and inference.

## Formalizability Standard

A concept is formalizable only if it can be expressed as explicit, testable logic without hidden discretionary judgment.

At minimum, it must:

- has identifiable boundaries;
- uses terms that can be defined;
- distinguishes conditions from conclusions;
- can be decomposed into observable components;
- remains coherent without unstated judgment.

## Evaluation and Status Rules

Each concept must be evaluated independently.

Combining multiple loosely defined ideas into a synthesized concept is not permitted unless the combination is explicitly defined by the source.

Formalization status must be one of the following:

- `candidate`: the concept is sufficiently defined from the source to proceed to formalization review;
- `unclear`: the concept cannot be unambiguously defined from the source material and must not be completed through inference;
- `rejected`: the concept does not satisfy the extraction or formalizability standard.

## Rejection Criteria

Reject or mark unclear when one or more of the following applies:

- the concept is too vague to define;
- the concept is underspecified or ungrounded in the source;
- the concept cannot be unambiguously defined from the source material;
- the concept depends on source authority;
- the concept is persuasive but not explicit;
- the concept changes meaning across examples without explicit criteria;
- the concept requires hidden discretionary judgment;
- the source context is insufficient to determine what is being claimed;
- the extracted item is only a prediction, recommendation, financial claim, or performance assertion.
